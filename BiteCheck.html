BiteCheck

Securing Allergy-Safe Snacks

Overview
BiteCheck is an iOS mobile application designed to assist individuals in finding allergy-safe snacks. Users are able to search snacks and see if they are allergy free, as well as create grocery lists.

BACKGROUND

As someone with severe allergies to peanut and tree nuts, I've had many experiences where I didn't know if I could eat a friends' snack or did not know whether I could buy a new snack while grocery shopping, especially if the ingredients are not in English.

I have also seen many others in awkward situation when it came to their allergies. My brother, who is also allergic to peanuts and tree nuts played house league soccer when he was young and at the end of the game when snacks were distributed, he could not always eat them due to his allergies. He would sometimes have to ask about the ingredients and felt bad when the parents apologized when he could not eat them.

While it might not seem hard to check ingredients, many people just forget or disregard their safety. I thought about how I could help, which is how I came up with Bitecheck, a digital solution for the digital age we live in.

While creating their account, users will be asked to input their allergies and when they search for snacks and if one of their allergies is found in the ingredients list, a pop-up overlay message will appear alerting the user. Upon searching a snack, they can access dedicated product pages where nutritional information (calories, total fat, protein, etc.) is listed along with its ingredients and fun facts. Aside from searching snacks, users can create lists, that mimic grocery lists. Upon searching snacks, users can add them to lists for different occasions. Lastly, users can read and/or create blogs on food topics ranging from recipes to tips on eating healthy and safe.

Problem
Individuals with food allergies do not check or read the ingredients list properly, take risks eating unfamiliar snacks and disregard the seriousness of their allergies


Solution
A Mobile application to be used by people of all ages with food allergies to provide a safer guidance system to obtain snacks and encourage the users to be more aware and minimize risks when snacking


USER RESEARCH

I spoke to various individuals with allergies ranging from sesame, to seafood, to peanuts and tree nuts. After discussing how they live their lives and eat safe, I drafted 2 personas that reflected two different target audiencces : individuals with allergies and parents who want snack suggestions for their kids with allergies.
