Project Voice

"Voice analysis tool to help you execute your best presentations!"


Overview
'Project Voice' is an android application designed to assist individuals of all ages in preparing for presentations by recording & outputing a variety of metrics through voice analysis.


Tools
Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator


Team Size
6 (1 PM, 4 Devs, 1 Design)


Role
Designer


Timeline
February 2019 - Current

Background

In the first 4 months of 2019, I had the privilege to co-op at Connected, a software product development firm based in downtown Toronto, where I met some amazing people. It did not take long until the group of 6 co-ops became close and couple moneths into our term, we decided to work together to create a product with an emphasis on the learning process.

The first step was to determine what everyone wanted to learn from this experience. Each of the co-ops came from different undergraduate majors where 4 were from computer science, 1 from ECE & 1 from SYDE. We first decided on a tech stack - Android Studio, to create an Android application. Next came the division of roles, where the majority of us would be developers. For instance, Steven had interest in learning how to organize a project and would become our PM. My interests in learning more about official design processes led me to become the lead designer and the rest would become developers. However, everyone would be free to work with someone in a different role (e.g. devlopers can design or assist the PM).

The project topic was the next item on the list. After brainstorming a variety of ideas, we saw one that would be enough of a challenge & allow us to learn different skills in design & development. In Startup culture, there are many opportunities for people to present and hold company-wide discussions/meetings. Connected embraces this culture and the addition of being a product development firm became the source of inspiration for a presentation aid.

We gave the project the codename 'Project Voice' and are continuing to work on it outside of company time. This page will cover my findings & learnings from the design process.

Research

In the past, I had a tendency to rush the research process & wanted to start wireframing as soon as possible. With only 4 months, we determined that the best course of action would be to design in parallel with development. I decided to start by drawing 2 basic user journeys - one covering the installation of the app while the other primarily covered app functionality.

The journeys helped me think of 'how might we' questions that I hoped to ask someone with extensive presentation experience. Steven & I set up a meeting with one of the company PMs to determine the potential uses for our idea & functionality considerations. This discussion became our primary research, which allowed us to determine the starting metrics for our application:

Shortly after, we drafted some task flows & determined how many screens would be needed & which ones to prioritize. Little did we know that we were completely off the mark at the time!

Planning & Wireframes

Shortly after drafting our ideas, I scheduled a meeting with a product designer at Connected and she gave us a lesson on breaking down task flows with edge cases, creating wireframes & user flows. After, I created a basic wireflow diagram and 2 primary task flows: new recording & viewing/editing past recordings.
