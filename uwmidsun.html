UW Midnight Sun Design Team

Pushing the Boundaries of Solar-Powered Transportation

Overview
Since 1988, the Midnight Sun Solar Car Team has been designing & building solar-powered electric vehicles at the University of Waterloo. We have competed at races around the world including the American Solar Challenge & The World Solar Challenge

With 3 other teammates, we set out to redesign the team website. What better way to show off a new car than with a new website?

Team Size
3 [3 Design]


Role
Web Designer


Timeline
September 2019 - Present


Tools
Squarespace, Figma, Photoshop, Trello

BACKGROUND

In preparation for the upcoming race in 2020, the business team has been busy gathering sponsors, organizing finances, recruiting new team members & redesigning promotional material. The projects two teammates & I took included recreating the team site & recruitment posters.

We created a new website using Squarespace but Why Squarespace? The idea behind using Squarespace is to make sure that the website is eay to maintain and add to in the future. The people who takeover in future years may not know how to code and so Squarespace seemed to be a suitable platform.

Planning

The 3 of us had only joined the team a few weeks ago and we needed to learn more about the team itself.

PROBLEM!
A UW Midsun Alumni was maintaining the website but unfortunately stopped & our website was deactivated! We were now on a time crunch & we needed to get our website up and running as soon as possible! Other UW Midnight Sun Business members have started reaching out to sponsors so we needed to have it up soon!


Solution (Temporary)
Our old website was dark, with a mysterious theme & we decided to incorporate that into our 'solution.' We designed & published a temporary landing page under the same domain, noting that a new website would be up and running soon. We had bought ourselves some time, but we were still on a tight schedule! Check out our landing page below:


Additionally, we were working together with the team's PM, who helped us prioritize the sites' content. We planned that we would set up the following before publishing:

  • Home
  • Sponsors
  • About Team
  • Car History

To ensure that multiple people did not take on the same tasks, we set up a Trello board. This also helped us see how our progress was and allowed us to break down our plans for each idea into stories.

After stories were written, it was up to the designers to design a quick mock-up of the idea and then receive feedback from core team members. We would also leave a note on the ticket with design specifications & reasons for certain decisions. See an image of our Trello board below!

Research

To learn more about the team, we spoke to core team members & observed members at work sessions on weekends. Together with sub-team leads & the PM, we compiled the necessary information in a Google Doc.

After that, we started looking at templates on Squarespace to get started. Personally, I was not familiar with modern website designs and the division of content between pages. Was it suitable for the home / landing page to include summaries of the team, goals, cars & sponsorship info or instead create a landing page with an image, a couple buttons and then have the user navigate to separate sub-pages?

Squarespace Limitations

While the Squarespace experience is designed to be fairly simple, it comes with numerous limitations, specifically ones relating to layout.

Spacers
Squarespace has a 'spacers' tool, which allows you to adjust padding by dragging and dropping blocks in 'edit' mode. However, there are very few sizes the spacers can be set to and this is the same for all other elements, including images. Unfortunately, due to the margins we created for each page, we were unable to centre a 3x3 grid of images. Additionally, the line height cannot be properly adjusted unless we embedded css into the text elements.


As seen in the image below, we were unable to properly centre-align the images nor edit line heights.


As I mentioned earlier, we are unbale to embed css into the site as future team members may not know the language and we may run into design inconsistencies. If I were to adjust the line height using CSS and future members do not utilize the same css code in new textual elements, our site will not longer be consistent.


Hence, it will be up to us to think of some creative solutions to solve these issues! I am currently investigating some quick fixes so look forward to some good news soon!
