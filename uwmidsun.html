Overview
Since 1988, the Midnight Sun Solar Car Team has been designing & building solar-powered electric vehicles at the University of Waterloo. We have competed at races around the world including the American Solar Challenge & The World Solar Challenge

With 3 other teammates, we set out to redesign the team website. What better way to show off a new car than with a new website?

Research

The 3 of us had only joined the team a few weeks ago and had to the team.

My first step was to research existing solutions - was there anyone who digitalized immunization records and if so, how can we take it one step further? Digital trackers we came across included the CDC mobile app, CANImmunize & Medscape.

Medscape stood out because it is a platform that allows Doctors & professionals to access patients' medical history. Aside from digital solutions, we took a look at different types of immunization records around the world butfocused on the ones in Ontario, Canada and a couple from the USA.

Planning & Wireframes

After spending a few hours researching, I quickly drew out an idea of how we would approach our idea. With one goal being to incorporate machine vision, we decided that the user would only have to take a photo of their immunization tracker cards using a camera function on a mobile application, which would then and then parse through the data & organize the infromation into a more readable table.

Second, we decided to create a web application linked to the mobile app, that would allow doctors and professionals to access these records. This web application would provide me the opportunity to try front-end development. Additionally with one of my friends, we would integrate the Django framework for data security and account purposes.

Before we started working, I sketched some UI ideas for the mobile app for our consumers and discussed what would be feasible and improvements that may be made later.
